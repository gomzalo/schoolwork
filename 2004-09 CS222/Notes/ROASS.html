Course Topics

    The principles behind machine languages and assembly language programming. Techniques for data structures, subprograms, parameters, recursion. A comparison of the two machines 68000 and SPARC.

Assignments and Labs

Expect five assignments and six labs. Assignments are due in class on the due date. Late assignments will not be accepted.

If you obtain less than 40% of the total possible marks on the assignment portion of the course, you fail the course. If you attend fewer than 5 labs, you grade will be lowered one level for each missing lab.

You will receive grades on some assignments prior to the voluntary withdrawal date. Generally, the professor will be happy to review marks on assignments, but any such requests should be made within two weeks of the handing back date.

Academic Honesty

Academic dishonesty is a very serious offence and will be dealt with in accordance with the University's discipline bylaw. Examples of academic dishonesty include: submitting assignments which are not entirely your own work, faking output that was not produced by your program, use of unauthorized material during a test or examination, writing an examination for another person or having another person write an examination for you. Do not post solutions to assignments or give your solutions to other students. Such actions may become plagiarism if those solutions are required in subsequent assignment questions.

A declaration sheet, which states that the work being submitted is completely your own, is available at http://www.cs.umanitoba.ca/newsite/honesty.html. This sheet must be printed out, filled in, signed, and attached to every assignment which is submitted. Any occurrence of plagiarism will be handled by the Associate Head of the Department of Computer Science.
